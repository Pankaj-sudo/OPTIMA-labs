Storage

Cold-chain, kept

Peptides are delicate. Keeping them cold and dark preserves their integrity from lab to last dose.

Arrives cold

Shipped with cold packs and insulated packaging so it reaches you at temperature.

Refrigerate reconstituted vials

Store mixed vials at 2–8 °C. Unmixed lyophilised powder is stable longer and can be frozen.

Away from light & heat

Keep out of direct sunlight and away from the freezer door. Avoid repeated temperature swings.

Preparation

Reconstitution, simplified

Mixing a peptide is simple when it's done gently and measured precisely.

1

Gather & sanitise

Peptide vial, bacteriostatic water, an alcohol swab and a sterile syringe on a clean surface.

2

Add water slowly

Draw your calculated volume of BAC water and let it run down the inside of the vial — never straight onto the powder.

3

Swirl, don't shake

Gently swirl until fully dissolved. Shaking can damage the peptide.

4

Label & date

Note the reconstitution date and concentration, then refrigerate. Use the calculator to get your exact units.

Handling & Administration

Safe, steady, simple

A few gentle habits keep every dose clean and comfortable.

Clean every time

Swab the vial stopper and injection site with alcohol before each use, and always use a fresh needle.

Rotate your sites

Alternate injection points to keep skin healthy and comfortable, and go slow and steady.

Dispose safely

Place used needles in a dedicated sharps container — never in household waste.

Questions

Frequently asked

How long does a reconstituted vial last?
Once mixed and refrigerated at 2–8 °C, most peptides remain stable for several weeks. Always follow your specific protocol and discard if the solution becomes cloudy or discoloured.
Can I freeze my peptides?
Unmixed lyophilised powder can be frozen for long-term storage. Once reconstituted, keep the vial refrigerated rather than frozen to preserve integrity.
How do I know how much to draw?
Our Peptide Calculator converts your peptide strength, desired dose and diluent volume into exact syringe units on a U-100 insulin syringe.
Why does everything need to stay cold?
Peptides are sensitive to heat and light. Cold-chain handling — from our lab through delivery to your fridge — protects their purity and potency.
